Network Analysis

This map analyzes emergency response accessibility from fire stations in Cascais using network analysis to evaluate service areas within 5, 10, 15, and 20 minutes (Thomas, 2022). It shows that many areas are accessible within 0 to 5 minutes, and all fire stations can reach the Sintra-Cascais Natural Park within 15 minutes for an initial attack. However, access to forested areas presents challenges for firefighting efforts, as indicated on the map.

Nature-Based Solutions

This project proposes nature-based solutions for the urbanized Fontainhas region in Porto, originally a rural area that became densely populated with worker housing in the 18th-19th centuries. Due to urban expansion and high-density development, Fontainhas now faces environmental challenges such as poor infrastructure, flooding, and inadequate green spaces. Key proposals include (a) Terraced Flood Management, (b) Building Renovations, (c) Community Spaces in Vacant Areas, (d) Green Parking and Rain Gardens, and (e) Enhanced Public Squares and Green Corridors, to improve local resilience, support climate adaptation, and foster community well-being by integrating green infrastructure throughout Fontainhas.
